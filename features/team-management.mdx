Gantt Maker's visual team management system uses color coding and intuitive interfaces to help you coordinate team members, assign tasks, and identify workload distribution at a glance.

## Overview

Every team member gets a unique color that follows them throughout the Gantt chart, making it instantly clear who is working on what and when.

<Tip>
  Visual color coding eliminates confusion in multi-person projects and helps identify resource conflicts immediately.
</Tip>

## Adding Team Members

Build your team with a simple, streamlined process:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open team management">
    Access the team management panel from your project configuration.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add a new member">
    Click "Add Member" and enter their name.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign color and role">
    Select a unique color for visual identification and specify their role or specialty.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="See them in the chart">
    The team member immediately appears in your Gantt chart, ready for task assignments.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  Each team member should have a distinct color to maximize visual clarity. The system helps ensure colors remain distinguishable.
</Note>

## Color Coding System

The color-based identification system is the cornerstone of Gantt Maker's visual approach:

### How Colors Work

- **Unique colors**: Each team member receives a distinct color
- **Persistent identification**: Colors follow members across all tasks and views
- **Visual scanning**: Quickly identify who owns which tasks without reading names
- **Conflict detection**: Spot over-allocation when one color appears too frequently
- **Status integration**: Colors combine with status indicators (pending, WIP, completed)

### Benefits of Visual Identification

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="For Project Managers">
    **Instant workload visibility**
    
    Scan the Gantt chart and immediately see:
    - Which team members are overloaded (too much of one color)
    - Who has capacity for additional tasks (underrepresented colors)
    - Timeline conflicts (same color in multiple simultaneous tasks)
    - Team distribution across project phases
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="For Team Members">
    **Clear personal responsibility**
    
    Team members can:
    - Instantly find their assigned tasks by color
    - Understand their workload at a glance
    - See how their work relates to others' timelines
    - Track personal progress through status indicators
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="For Stakeholders">
    **Transparent resource allocation**
    
    Stakeholders benefit from:
    - Quick understanding of team distribution
    - Visual confirmation of resource investment
    - Clear accountability for each project phase
    - Easy identification of bottlenecks
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Drag-and-Drop Reassignments

Change task assignments with intuitive drag-and-drop interactions:

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Select a task">
    Click on any task bar in your Gantt chart.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Drag to reassign">
    Drag the task to a different team member's row or use the assignment controls.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="See instant updates">
    The task's color changes immediately to reflect the new assignment.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Verify workload">
    Check that the new assignment doesn't create conflicts or overload.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Reassigning tasks updates the visual representation in real-time, making it easy to experiment with different resource allocations.
</Tip>

## Team Management Best Practices

### Choosing Colors

- **High contrast**: Select colors that are easily distinguishable
- **Accessibility**: Consider colorblind-friendly palettes
- **Consistency**: Keep colors stable throughout project lifecycle
- **Team input**: Let members choose their own colors when possible

### Defining Roles

- **Be specific**: "Senior Frontend Developer" is better than "Developer"
- **Include skills**: Add key technologies or specialties
- **Update regularly**: Reflect team growth and skill development
- **Align with tasks**: Ensure role definitions match task requirements

### Managing Workload

<Note>
  Use the visual color distribution across your timeline to ensure no single team member (color) dominates the schedule, indicating potential overallocation.
</Note>

- Scan for color balance across timeline periods
- Look for simultaneous assignments (same color, overlapping times)
- Consider skill overlap for backup assignments
- Plan for vacation and time-off by adjusting color distributions
